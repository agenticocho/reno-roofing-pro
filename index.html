Frequently Asked

Quick answers about re-roofs, replacements, and recovers in Reno and Sparks.

What's the difference between a re-roof and a recover?

A re-roof (tear-off) removes all existing materials down to the decking before installing a new system. A recover (overlay) installs new shingles directly over the old layer — faster and less expensive when the decking is sound. Text or call for an on-site assessment.

How long does a roof replacement take in Reno?

Most residential replacements in Reno and Sparks take 1–2 days. Larger homes or complex pitches may take 2–3 days. Text (775) 432-8833 for a timeline.

What is a certified master shingler?

A master shingler is an industry-recognized credential earned after years of hands-on roofing installation. It signals advanced technical skill — not just basic installation. 9 of this roofer's 21 years have been at master shingler level.
